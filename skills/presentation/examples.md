# Presentation — Real Usage Examples

*Copy these prompts directly into your AI agent to get started.*

---

## Quick Start Prompts

### Build From Scratch

"I need to present our Q3 results to the executive team next Friday. The numbers are mixed — some good, some not. Help me build a narrative that is honest but constructive."

### Fix an Existing Deck

"I have a 40-slide deck that I need to cut to 15 minutes. Help me figure out what stays and what goes."

### Prepare for a High-Stakes Pitch

"I am pitching to investors for the first time next week. I have a product that works but I have never done this before. Where do I start?"

### Handle a Difficult Topic

"I need to present a restructuring plan to my team that includes layoffs. Help me structure this so it is honest, humane, and clear."

## Conversation Examples

### Example 1: The Data-Heavy Presentation

User: I have 6 months of customer research data and I need to present findings to leadership in 20 minutes. I do not know where to start.

Agent: Start with the one thing leadership needs to do differently based on what you found. Everything else in the presentation exists to support that recommendation. What is the single most important finding from your research — the one that changes something? Start there and we will build backward from it. The data is evidence for that conclusion, not the presentation itself.

---

### Example 2: The Skeptical Audience

User: I am presenting a new initiative that I know some senior stakeholders are skeptical about. How do I handle the resistance?

Agent: Address the skepticism directly rather than hoping it does not come up. Build a slide that names the most common objection — not dismissively, but genuinely — and then answers it with your strongest evidence. Audiences respect presenters who have thought through the counterarguments. What is the objection you are most worried about? Let us build your response to it before we build anything else.

---

### Example 3: The Keynote

User: I am giving a 45-minute keynote at an industry conference. The topic is the future of my field. I have ideas but no structure.

Agent: For a 45-minute keynote the structure that works is: open with the problem or tension that makes this moment important, spend the middle building the case for a specific point of view, and close with what this means for the people in the room. You are not summarizing the field — you are making an argument about it. What is the thing you believe about the future of your field that most people in that room do not yet believe? That is your keynote.

---

### Example 4: Presentation Anxiety

User: I am terrified of public speaking. I have a team presentation in two weeks and I cannot sleep thinking about it.

Agent: The anxiety is almost always about the gap between preparation and performance — the sense that something unexpected will happen and you will not know how to handle it. The most effective thing you can do in two weeks is close that gap. Not by rehearsing until the presentation feels scripted, but by knowing your material well enough that no question surprises you and no technical issue derails you. Tell me about the presentation and we will start with the parts that feel most uncertain.

## Power User Prompts

"Write an opening for my presentation that creates genuine tension in the first 30 seconds without being manipulative."

"I have 8 minutes and 12 slides. Tell me which slides to cut and why."

"My last presentation went flat halfway through. Here is the structure I used: [outline]. Where did I lose them?"

"Generate the 10 hardest questions my audience could ask about this presentation and help me prepare answers."

"Write speaker notes for this slide that sound like me talking, not like me reading: [slide content]."

---

*A presentation is not a document read aloud. It is a conversation with a room. Prepare for the conversation.*
